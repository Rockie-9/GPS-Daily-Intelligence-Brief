When rivals rewrite the rules, GPS guards resilience through change, not a single advantage

2026 年 5 月 25 日，華為半導體業務總裁何庭波在上海 ISCAS 研討會發表「韜（τ）定律」，宣稱以「時間縮微」取代「幾何縮微」，透過 3D 邏輯折疊在垂直空間堆疊電路，目標 2031 年達到 1.4 奈米製程同等電晶體密度。麒麟 2026 試驗晶片密度已提升 53.5%。消息驅動 A 股科創 50 指數當日大漲 5.88%，隔日卻全線回落——市場與專家對能否突破散熱、良率、功耗仍存疑。
On May 25, 2026, Huawei semiconductor chief He Tingbo unveiled the "Tau Law" at Shanghai's ISCAS symposium, claiming to replace "geometric scaling" with "time scaling" — stacking circuits vertically via 3D logic folding, targeting transistor density equivalent to 1.4nm by 2031. The Kirin 2026 test chip already shows 53.5% density gain. The news drove China's STAR 50 index up 5.88% that day, then fell back the next — markets and experts still doubt whether it overcomes heat, yield, and power challenges.
為什麼這是今日主信號：韜定律真假未定，但它揭示一件事：競爭格局可能從「製程領先」轉向「系統架構整合」——遊戲規則正在被改寫。GPS 不在技術競爭的第一線，但 GPS 守護的是讓技術競爭得以發生的底層條件——人才的安心、營運的穩定、機密的保全。當外部規則劇變，內部的安全韌性反而是不變的根基。
Why this is today's lead: The Tau Law's validity is unproven, but it reveals one thing: competition may shift from "process leadership" toward "system-architecture integration" — the rules are being rewritten. GPS isn't on the front line of tech competition, but GPS guards the underlying conditions that let tech competition happen — talent's peace of mind, operational stability, secret protection. When external rules shift drastically, internal security resilience is the unchanging foundation.
GPS 透鏡
GPS LENS
韜定律的真正啟示不在技術細節，在「沒有一家企業能獨自完成所有答案」這句話——連何庭波都強調開放合作。安全也是如此：當競爭從單點突破轉向系統整合，GPS 的價值不在守住某一道牆，而在讓整個組織成為一個能協同應變的系統。製程會被追趕、優勢會被複製，但「組織如何在變局中持續守護人」這種韌性，是無法被抄走的。
The Tau Law's real lesson isn't in technical detail but in one line — "no single company can complete all the answers alone" — even He Tingbo stressed open collaboration. Security is the same: when competition shifts from single breakthroughs to system integration, GPS's value isn't in holding one wall but in making the whole organization a system that can respond in concert. Process can be caught up to, advantages copied — but the resilience of "how an organization keeps guarding people through upheaval" cannot be stolen.

「製程可以被追趕，優勢可以被複製，但一個組織在變局中持續守護人的能力，是抄不走的護城河。」

Semiconductor Watch · Story 015 SOURCES
華為「韜定律」：一場關於「誰定義競爭」的戰爭
Huawei's "Tau Law": a war over who defines competition

事實：2026/05/25，華為董事、半導體業務總裁何庭波在上海 ISCAS 2026 研討會發表「韜（τ）定律」，主張以「時間縮微」取代摩爾定律的「幾何縮微」，透過 3D 邏輯折疊在垂直空間多層堆疊電路，降低訊號傳播時間常數。華為稱 6 年來已量產 381 款晶片，麒麟 2026 試驗晶片密度提升 53.5%，目標 2031 年達 1.4 奈米同等電晶體密度。消息驅動 A 股科創 50 當日漲 5.88%，隔日全市場 4077 檔下跌、半導體領跌。

為什麼重要：路透與 CNBC 均指出，外界質疑大陸在美國光刻設備管制下能否真正達標，散熱、良率、功耗都是疑問。但這件事的重點不在技術真假，在它挑戰的對象——台積電賴以領先的「製程微縮」路徑。韜定律若成立，競爭格局可能從「誰的製程更小」轉向「誰的系統整合更強」。這是定義權之爭：誰能定義「下一輪競爭比什麼」，誰就握有戰略主動。

What happened: On 2026/05/25, Huawei board member and semiconductor chief He Tingbo unveiled the "Tau (τ) Law" at Shanghai's ISCAS 2026, proposing "time scaling" to replace Moore's Law's "geometric scaling" — stacking circuits vertically via 3D logic folding to reduce signal time constants. Huawei claims 381 chips mass-produced over 6 years; the Kirin 2026 test chip shows 53.5% density gain, targeting 1.4nm-equivalent density by 2031. The news drove China's STAR 50 up 5.88% that day; the next day 4,077 stocks fell, semiconductors leading the drop.

Why it matters: Reuters and CNBC both note skepticism over whether China can truly hit the target under US lithography controls — heat, yield, and power remain open questions. But the point isn't the tech's validity; it's what it challenges — the "process scaling" path TSMC leads on. If the Tau Law holds, competition may shift from "whose process is smaller" to "whose system integration is stronger." This is a contest over definitional power: whoever defines "what the next round competes on" holds the strategic initiative.

GPS 解讀：對 GPS 而言，這個訊號的啟示是抽象但深刻的：當外部競爭規則可能被改寫，組織不能只依賴單一優勢。何庭波自己說「沒有一家企業能獨自完成所有答案」——這句話對安全同樣成立。GPS 的韌性不該建立在某一道牆、某一套系統，而在於整個組織能否協同應變。製程會被追趕，但「組織在變局中持續守護人」的能力，是無法被複製的。
GPS Lens: For GPS, the lesson is abstract but profound: when external rules may be rewritten, an organization cannot rely on a single advantage. He Tingbo himself said "no single company can complete all the answers alone" — equally true for security. GPS's resilience shouldn't rest on one wall or one system, but on whether the whole organization can respond in concert. Process gets caught up to, but the ability to "keep guarding people through upheaval" cannot be copied.
建議行動：這不是要你今天做什麼，是換一個思考框架：盤點 GPS 目前的「單點依賴」——是否過度依賴某一套監控系統、某一個關鍵人員、某一道門禁？真正的韌性不在單點最強，在系統能補位。下次規劃時，多問一句：「如果這一點失效，整個系統還能運作嗎？」
Recommended action: This isn't asking you to act today; it's a reframe: inventory GPS's "single-point dependencies" — are we over-reliant on one surveillance system, one key person, one access gate? Real resilience isn't in the strongest single point but in the system's ability to compensate. Next time you plan, ask one more question: "If this point fails, can the whole system still run?"
可轉述句：「製程可以被追趕，優勢可以被複製，但一個組織在變局中持續守護人的能力，是抄不走的護城河。」
Cascade sentence: "Process can be caught up to, advantages copied — but an organization's ability to keep guarding people through upheaval is a moat that cannot be stolen."

Industry Watch · Story 022 SOURCES
黃仁勳拜訪張忠謀：產業合縱連橫，安全是夥伴關係的隱形基礎
Jensen Huang visits Morris Chang: as alliances form, security is the invisible foundation

事實：2026/05/25，NVIDIA 執行長黃仁勳訪台期間拜訪台積電創辦人張忠謀。在 AI 晶片需求持續高漲、供應鏈夥伴關係日益緊密的背景下，這場會晤象徵半導體與 AI 產業的合縱連橫持續深化。同期 Sony 與台積電簽署次世代影像感測器戰略合作基本協議、熊本 JASM 廠 Q1 轉虧為盈。

為什麼重要：產業合作越緊密，跨組織的人員、資訊、實體流動就越頻繁——這正是實體安全的新挑戰。當外部夥伴頻繁進出廠區、當合作專案需要共享敏感資訊，傳統「築牆防守」的思維不再適用。安全必須從「阻擋外人」轉向「設計可信任的協作流程」。

What happened: On 2026/05/25, NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang visited TSMC founder Morris Chang during his Taiwan trip. Against surging AI-chip demand and tightening supply-chain partnerships, the meeting signals deepening alignment across the semiconductor and AI industries. In the same period, Sony and TSMC signed a basic agreement on next-gen image-sensor cooperation; Kumamoto JASM turned profitable in Q1.

Why it matters: The tighter industry collaboration, the more frequent the cross-organizational flow of people, information, and physical access — exactly the new challenge for physical security. When external partners frequently enter sites and joint projects require sharing sensitive information, the traditional "build walls" mindset no longer fits. Security must shift from "blocking outsiders" to "designing trustworthy collaboration processes."

GPS 解讀：GPS Pillar 02 旅程守護的範疇，早已從「台積同仁」延伸到「供應鏈夥伴、外部訪客」。黃仁勳這類高規格訪問，是 GPS 展現「體貼的實體安全夥伴」定位的時刻——安全不是讓訪客感到被監視，而是讓他們在抵達前，動線、接待、資訊存取都已被妥善設計。最好的安全，是訪客感受不到摩擦，卻全程被守護。
GPS Lens: GPS Pillar 02 Journey Protection has long extended from "TSMCers" to "supply-chain partners and external visitors." High-profile visits like Huang's are moments for GPS to embody its "considerate physical security partner" positioning — security isn't making visitors feel watched, but ensuring their route, reception, and information access are well-designed before they arrive. The best security: visitors feel no friction yet are guarded throughout.
建議行動：換一個視角看「訪客接待」：它不是行政庶務，是 GPS 的品牌時刻。下次有重要外賓來訪，問一句：「這位訪客從抵達到離開，會不會在任何一刻感到被當成威脅、而非貴賓？」安全與禮遇不該衝突，好的設計能兩者兼得。
Recommended action: Reframe "visitor reception": it's not administrative chores but a GPS brand moment. Next time an important guest visits, ask: "From arrival to departure, will this visitor at any moment feel treated as a threat rather than an honored guest?" Security and hospitality needn't conflict — good design achieves both.

Resilience Watch · Story 032 SOURCES · OFFICIAL
颱風薔蜜逼近 + 水情吃緊：營運韌性的雙重壓力測試
Typhoon Sepat + tight water supply: a double stress test for operational resilience

事實：中央氣象署預測，第六號颱風薔蜜將於今日（5/31）增強為中度颱風，向北直衝日本，外圍環流明日（6/1）影響台灣北部及宜蘭；下週五（6/5）低壓風帶與鋒面雨勢將擴及全台。與此同時水情吃緊——全台累積降雨量僅達歷史同期 6 成，南部山區更只有 3 至 6 成，呈「北多南少」。氣象署長指出「真正的梅雨季其實還沒到」。

為什麼重要：半導體製造對水、電極度敏感——缺水影響晶圓清洗，颱風威脅電力與廠房安全。這是 GPS 與廠務、環安衛交集的典型場景。但對 GPS 而言，颱風的挑戰不只在硬體防護，更在「人」：員工通勤安全、廠區留守人員調度、緊急應變時的資訊傳遞。

What happened: Taiwan's CWA forecasts the sixth typhoon, Sepat, intensifying to moderate strength today (5/31), heading north toward Japan; its outer bands affect northern Taiwan and Yilan tomorrow (6/1); next Friday (6/5) a low-pressure belt and front bring rain nationwide. Meanwhile water supply is tight — cumulative rainfall is only 60% of the historical norm, southern mountains just 30-60%, a "wetter north, drier south" pattern. The CWA chief noted "the real plum-rain season hasn't arrived yet."

Why it matters: Semiconductor manufacturing is extremely sensitive to water and power — shortages affect wafer cleaning, typhoons threaten power and facility safety. This is a classic intersection of GPS with facilities and EHS. But for GPS, the typhoon challenge isn't only hardware protection — it's "people": employee commute safety, on-site standby staffing, information relay during emergencies.

GPS 解讀：颱風是可預測的——這正是 GPS「在您需要之前」品牌精神的最佳展演場。被動的安全是颱風來了才應變；主動的安全是在外圍環流抵達前，員工已收到清楚的通勤指引、留守安排已定、緊急聯絡網已測試。同樣的氣象資訊，可以只是「災防通知」，也可以是「讓同仁感到被照顧」的關懷觸點——差別在於用什麼語氣、提前多久、是否設身處地。
GPS Lens: Typhoons are predictable — exactly the best showcase for GPS's "Before you need it" brand spirit. Passive security responds after the typhoon arrives; proactive security ensures that before the outer bands hit, employees have clear commute guidance, standby arrangements are set, and emergency contact networks are tested. The same weather data can be merely a "disaster alert," or a "care touchpoint that makes colleagues feel looked after" — the difference is tone, lead time, and empathy.
建議行動：趁颱風還沒到，做一次「提前量」練習：如果明天外圍環流就到，你負責範圍內的同仁，需要知道什麼？（通勤調整、留守安排、緊急聯絡）把它寫成一則 3 句話的關懷訊息，語氣是「我們已經為您想好」，而非「請注意安全」。提前、具體、設身處地——這就是品牌精神的日常實踐。
Recommended action: Before the typhoon arrives, practice "lead time": if the outer bands hit tomorrow, what do the colleagues in your area need to know? (Commute adjustments, standby arrangements, emergency contacts.) Write it as a 3-sentence care message, with a tone of "we've thought this through for you," not "please stay safe." Early, specific, empathetic — that's the brand spirit in daily practice.
可轉述句：「颱風是可預測的——被動的安全等它來，主動的安全在它來之前，就已讓同仁感到被照顧。」
Cascade sentence: "Typhoons are predictable — passive security waits for it; proactive security makes colleagues feel cared for before it arrives."

Industry Watch · Story 042 SOURCES
半導體業資安保險投保奪冠——成熟風險心態的標竿
Semiconductor leads in cyber insurance — a benchmark for mature risk mindset

事實：金管會統計，截至 2025 年底台灣資安保險保費收入達 5.12 億元，連續三年逾 5 億。其中半導體業投保金額約 1.48 億元、占比 28.87% 居冠，其次為電腦周邊（1.25 億）、金融保險（約 1 億）。2025 年理賠金額僅 793 萬元——業者指出企業投保前須經嚴格核保（資安防護、第三方稽核、營運持續計畫），已具一定防禦能力。

為什麼重要：半導體業在資安保險的領先，反映成熟的風險管理心態：不是「我們不會被攻擊」，而是「我們假設會被攻擊，並為殘餘風險做財務準備」。這個心態正是 GPS 可借鏡並延伸到實體安全領域的——保險核保要求的「第三方稽核 + 營運持續計畫」，本質上就是 GPS 的工作範疇。

What happened: Taiwan's FSC reports cyber insurance premiums reached NT$512M by end-2025, exceeding NT$500M for three years. Semiconductor leads at ~NT$148M (28.87%), then computer peripherals (NT$125M) and finance (~NT$100M). 2025 claims totaled only NT$7.93M — insurers note firms must pass rigorous underwriting (security measures, third-party audits, business continuity plans) before coverage.

Why it matters: Semiconductor's insurance lead reflects a mature risk mindset: not "we won't be attacked," but "we assume we will be, and prepare financially for residual risk." This is exactly what GPS can borrow for physical security — the "third-party audit + business continuity plan" required for underwriting is essentially GPS's remit.

GPS 解讀：借用「保險核保邏輯」做一次 GPS 自我盤點：如果有一份「實體安全保險」要為我們的廠區核保，核保人會問什麼？（門禁完整性、監控覆蓋率、應變計畫、稽核紀錄）把這些問題列出來，本身就是一份 GPS 成熟度自評表。這框架對主管層特別有說服力，因為它用財務語言講安全。
GPS Lens: Borrow "underwriting logic" for a GPS self-assessment: if a "physical security insurance" underwrote our sites, what would the underwriter ask? (Access integrity, surveillance coverage, response plans, audit records.) Listing these is itself a GPS maturity self-assessment — especially persuasive to leadership because it speaks security in financial language.
建議行動：換一個視角看資產保護：保險業用「核保問卷」量化風險，GPS 也可以。試著想像一份「實體安全核保問卷」會問什麼——門禁、監控、應變、稽核。能回答得越完整，代表 GPS 成熟度越高。缺口就是下一季的改善方向。
Recommended action: Reframe asset protection: insurers quantify risk via "underwriting questionnaires" — GPS can too. Imagine what a "physical security underwriting questionnaire" would ask — access, surveillance, response, audit. The more completely you can answer, the higher GPS's maturity. Gaps become next quarter's improvement direction.
可轉述句：「成熟的安全不是承諾零風險，是誠實面對殘餘風險並為它做準備。」
Cascade sentence: "Mature security isn't promising zero risk — it's honestly facing residual risk and preparing for it."

People Safety Watch · Story 052 SOURCES
假冒主管 LINE 詐騙在職場擴散——查證文化是最好的防線
Fake-boss LINE scams spread in workplaces — a verification culture is the best defense

事實：內政部 165 反詐騙專線多次發布警示，假冒公司主管的 LINE 訊息詐騙在職場持續擴散：詐騙者冒用主管名義建立 LINE 群組或私訊員工，以「急需匯款」、「代購禮品卡」、「處理機密交易」等話術誘騙財務或行政人員。手法關鍵在於利用組織層級壓力——員工接到「主管」指示時，傾向先服從、後查證。

為什麼重要：詐騙能得逞，根因不是員工不夠聰明，是組織文化讓人不敢質疑權威。這是 GPS Pillar 06 人身安全最貼近員工日常的場景，也是「文化」而非「技術」的問題——正是 GPS 理解組織文化的優勢所在。

What happened: Taiwan's 165 anti-fraud hotline repeatedly warns as fake-boss LINE scams spread in workplaces: scammers impersonate executives via LINE groups or DMs, using scripts like "urgent transfer," "buy gift cards," "handle a confidential transaction" to deceive finance or admin staff. The key is exploiting hierarchy pressure — when employees receive instructions from "an executive," they comply first, verify later.

Why it matters: Scams succeed not because employees aren't smart, but because culture makes them afraid to question authority. This is the scenario closest to daily life under GPS Pillar 06 Personal Safety, and a "culture" not "technical" problem — exactly where GPS's cultural understanding is an advantage.

GPS 解讀：真正的安全文化，是讓「查證」變成被鼓勵、而非被質疑的行為。製作一張極簡「停看聽」卡片：收到主管異常指示（匯款、買卡、機密交易）時，停（正常公務不會用私人 LINE 急迫要錢）、看（是否有壓力話術）、聽（用另一個已知管道向本人查證）。關鍵訊息：「查證不會冒犯主管，被騙才會。」
GPS Lens: A real safety culture makes "verifying" an encouraged act, not a doubted one. Create a minimal "Stop-Look-Listen" card: on receiving abnormal executive instructions (transfers, cards, confidential transactions), Stop (normal business won't urgently demand money via personal LINE), Look (any pressure scripts?), Listen (verify with the person via another known channel). Key message: "Verifying won't offend the executive — being scammed will."
建議行動：這個觀念可以變成行動：把「停看聽」做成一頁卡片，推送給財務、行政、採購等高風險族群。卡片不需長，核心是給員工一個「不會被究責的查證理由」。
Recommended action: This idea can become action: turn "Stop-Look-Listen" into a one-page card, push to high-risk groups like finance, admin, procurement. The card need not be long — its core is giving employees a "blame-free reason to verify."
可轉述句：「我們提醒同仁查證，不是因為不信任主管——是因為我們知道，真正的主管永遠不介意被查證。會介意的，才是該擔心的。」
Cascade sentence: "We remind colleagues to verify not because we distrust executives — but because a real executive never minds being verified. The ones who mind are the ones to worry about."

08a · 今日一個策略思考
08a · Today's Strategic Reflection
當對手改寫規則，韌性不在單點最強
When rivals rewrite the rules, resilience isn't in one strong point

華為韜定律的真正啟示不在技術，在「沒有一家企業能獨自完成所有答案」。安全也是如此：當競爭規則可能被改寫，GPS 的韌性不該押注在單一優勢。盤點你負責範圍的「單點依賴」——某套系統、某個人、某道門。下次規劃時多問一句：「如果這一點失效，整個系統還能運作嗎？」

The Tau Law's real lesson isn't technical but this: "no single company can complete all the answers alone." Security is the same — when rules may be rewritten, GPS resilience shouldn't bet on a single advantage. Inventory the "single-point dependencies" in your area — one system, one person, one gate. Next time you plan, ask: "If this point fails, can the whole system still run?"
